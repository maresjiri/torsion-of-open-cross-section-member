<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">for both ends, with
a value of 0.7 for one end fixed and the other hinged.</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm"><br/>

</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">The coefficient ky
refers to the end rotation in the plane perpendicular to the y-y
axis, the coefficient kz refers to to the end rotation in the plane
perpendicular to the z-axis. These coefficients are analogous to the
ratio Lcr/L of a compressed member. The factor kw relates to the end
warping. Unless a special measure to prevent warping is applied, k =
1.0 may be taken.</p>

<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">The sign convention
for determining z and zj, see Figure NB.3.1, is:</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">[1] the z coordinate
is positive for the compressed flange. When zj is determined using
the relation in NB.3.2(1), it points the z-axis upwards in the case
of a gravity load. The z-axis points downwards in the case of a
suction load;</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">[2] the sign of zj
is the same as the sign of the cross-sectional asymmetry coefficient
ψ</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm"><br/>

</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">The sign convention
for zg is:</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">[1] for load effects
zg is positive when the load is applied above the centre of shear;</p>
<p style="line-height: 100%; margin-bottom: 0mm">[2] in the general
case zg is positive when the load is directed from the point of
action to the centre of shear</p>
